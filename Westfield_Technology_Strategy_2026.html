Purpose

This document is Westfield's 2026 Technology Strategy and Operating Model. It consolidates the strategic plan update, operating governance, domain leadership structure, CORE charter alignment, and AI initiatives — including Claude — into a single reference for firm leadership.

Claude is live across claude.ai, Claude in Excel, Cowork, and Claude Code. This document places the Claude deployment in its proper context: one initiative within a broader strategy, governed by the same operating model and accountability structures that apply to all Technology work.

Guiding Principle — aligned to CORE Charter

Commit only to what we can execute — and execute what we commit to.

Claude is on the Commitments Board. It has named owners, a phased rollout sequence, and explicit trade-offs against capacity. It is governed like every other firm-level commitment — not differently because it is AI.

02

What Claude Is Not

Before expanding firm-wide, it is important to define boundaries explicitly. Without them, scope creep and misuse are predictable outcomes.

  • Not a research replacement. Bloomberg, AlphaSense, and FactSet remain the authoritative sources for market data and investment research. Claude assists with synthesis, drafting, and summarization — it does not generate investment recommendations as a primary source.
  • Not a compliance-free zone. All AI-assisted work products that inform investment decisions remain subject to existing compliance review workflows. Claude does not bypass the Marketing Rule, pre-trade compliance, or any other regulatory obligation.
  • Not an approved channel for sensitive data. MNPI, unmasked client PII, and confidential counterparty information may not be entered into Claude products until explicit data handling controls are confirmed and documented in the AUP.
  • Not a substitute for firm systems of record. Work products generated with Claude assistance must be saved and retained in approved systems (Box, Global Relay, OMS). Claude itself is not a system of record.
  • Claude Code is not approved for production deployments without standard review. Code generated with Claude Code assistance follows existing code review, QA, and deployment processes — it does not skip the pipeline.

04

Alignment to the 2026 Technology Operating Model

4.1  Claude as MCP Integration Preview

The Operating Model sequences MCP as the integration layer before AI proliferates. Claude in production is a preview of that model — and a warning. It demonstrates the value of governed AI access while creating urgency: Claude agents must eventually operate on clean, auditable MCP data flows, not uncontrolled side channels.

Risk if MCP slips

If Claude adoption accelerates while MCP integration stalls, users will route around the governed data layer — pulling data manually into prompts, creating audit gaps, and building habits that are hard to unwind.

MCP must stay protected on the CORE Commitments Board and be treated as a dependency of Claude Phase 3, not a parallel workstream.

Governance Requirements

The Claude deployment introduces governance requirements not covered by the 2025 Strategic Plan. Two of these are gate items — Claude does not expand to the full firm population until they are closed.

7.1  AI Acceptable Use Policy  GATE ITEM — Q1 2026

Owner: CTO (Dee Silveira). Review: CCO. Claude cannot expand beyond the current pilot population until this is published and acknowledged firm-wide.

The AUP must address:

  • Data classification boundaries — what may and may not be entered into each Claude product
  • Approved use cases — which workflows are sanctioned by function and product tier
  • Prohibited uses — investment recommendations as primary source; MNPI or unmasked client PII; personal accounts for firm work
  • Work product handling — how Claude-assisted outputs are saved, attributed, and retained in firm systems
  • Incident reporting — what constitutes a data handling incident and how to escalate
7.2  Vendor Due Diligence  GATE ITEM — Q1 2026

Owner: CTO (Dee Silveira). Review: CCO and Legal. Claude cannot expand firm-wide until Anthropic's data processing agreement is confirmed under Westfield's vendor governance framework.

Specific risks requiring CCO assessment and documented position:

SEC Marketing Rule. AI-assisted client communications are subject to existing review workflows without exception. Define the review step explicitly in the AUP — Claude does not create a new approval path.
MNPI Protocols. Analysts must confirm no material non-public information is entered into cloud-hosted AI tools. This is a behavioral control; the AUP must state it explicitly and training must reinforce it.
Books and Records. Determine whether Claude-generated work products that inform investment decisions constitute records subject to retention policy. A documented CCO position is required before broad investment team usage.
Data Processing Agreement. Confirm Anthropic's enterprise terms are in place — specifically that Westfield inputs are not used for model training. Classify claude.ai as a SaaS vendor in Westfield's vendor risk register.
7.3  Ongoing Controls (post-gate)
  • DLP rules to flag or block sensitive data patterns entering Claude products — implemented by Juan Matias in coordination with security tooling
  • Audit logging for Claude usage maintained alongside other SaaS audit trails
  • Annual AUP review cycle, with interim updates triggered by material changes in Claude product capabilities or regulatory guidance

Decisions Requested from Leadership

The following decisions gate Claude's progress from Phase 1 to Phase 2. They are sequenced by urgency.

Gate Items — Required Before Phase 2 Expansion
  1. CCO and COO endorse the AI Acceptable Use Policy — without this, claude.ai does not expand beyond the current pilot population.
  2. CCO confirms Anthropic vendor due diligence is complete — DPA confirmed, vendor risk register updated, books-and-records position documented.

Additional decisions required to proceed with the full operating model:

  • Confirm MCP stays protected on the CORE Commitments Board — approve that MCP integration is a dependency of Claude Phase 3, not a discretionary parallel workstream that can be deferred without consequence
  • Confirm CTO authority for Claude architecture and deployment — Dee Silveira is the named decision-maker for Claude strategy and architecture; domain leads are the contacts for their respective workstreams, consistent with the January 2026 Domain Leadership Memo
  • Endorse four-phase rollout sequence as the agreed execution direction via CORE — no phase begins without prior phase gate items closed
  • Approve budget for Claude Code licenses and any additional claude.ai seats required for Phase 2 expansion
11

Closing

Claude is Westfield's most visible AI deployment to date. It shifts the Technology narrative from preparing for AI to operating with AI — and it does so under the governance structures the firm has deliberately built: shared demand management, distributed domain ownership, and explicit capacity trade-offs through CORE.

The measures of success here are not headline metrics. They are the same measures we apply to everything else: committed work delivered on time, zero incidents that could have been prevented by governance, domain leads recognized as decision-makers, and business partners who find Technology easy to engage. Claude is one part of a coherent operating model — not a standalone initiative.
