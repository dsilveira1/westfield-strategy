01 · Risk Register

Known risks.
Active mitigations.

Critical
AI Output Used Without Verification
Client-facing materials generated by Claude could contain errors, hallucinations, or outdated information if published without human review. Regulatory and reputational exposure if AI-generated analysis is treated as authoritative without oversight.
Mitigation
All AI-generated client outputs require analyst sign-off before distribution. Airia MCP governance layer logs every query and response. Human-in-the-loop mandate written into sprint requirements.
Critical
Sensitive Data Exposure via Prompts
Portfolio positions, client information, and proprietary investment thesis could be inadvertently included in prompts sent to external AI services. Data residency and confidentiality obligations at risk.
Mitigation
Anthropic enterprise agreement with data privacy terms in place. Airia governance layer intercepts and masks sensitive identifiers before queries leave the perimeter. Prompt templates audited quarterly.
Moderate
Model Dependency & Vendor Lock-in
Deep integration with Claude+MCP creates operational dependency on Anthropic's API availability and pricing. Model capability changes in future versions could disrupt established workflows.
Mitigation
MCP architecture is model-agnostic by design. Abstraction layer between Airia and Claude allows swap to alternative models. SLA monitoring and fallback procedures documented in sprint governance.
Moderate
Team Skill Gap & Adoption Resistance
Productivity gains depend on analysts actually using Claude effectively. Poor prompting hygiene, distrust of AI outputs, or workflow friction could limit realized value below projected gains.
Mitigation
Phase 3 (Resource) dedicated to training and workflow integration. Champions identified in each team. Early wins documented and shared to build confidence. Feedback loops built into sprint cadence.
Moderate
Regulatory Uncertainty — AI in Finance
SEC, FINRA, and state regulations around AI-generated investment communications are evolving rapidly. What is permissible today may require disclosure or prohibition tomorrow.
Mitigation
Compliance team engaged in sprint governance from Phase 1. AI-generated content flagged and archived per standard document retention. Legal counsel reviewing AI use policy in parallel with sprint execution.
Managed
Infrastructure Integration Complexity
Connecting Eze/APX → Snowflake → Airia → Claude requires multiple secure data pipelines. Integration failures could produce stale or incorrect data in AI responses.
Mitigation
18 months of infrastructure modernization provides stable foundation. Eze upgrade (March) completes the critical dependency. Data quality checks at each pipeline stage before Claude receives context.

05 · Our Position

Strategic assessment

Westfield enters this sprint from a position of genuine infrastructure strength — 18 months of modernization means we're not building the foundation while trying to deploy AI. The critical gaps are in governance and output quality control, both of which are known and being addressed in Phase 3. The firm is ready to build, not yet ready to scale to clients — and that sequencing is correct.
